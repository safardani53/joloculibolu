Nikita Kucherov | Early Hockey Career

Talking about his early career, Kucherov professionally played for CSKA Moscow, the Kontinental Hockey League(KHL). Then the Tampa Bay Lightning selected the Russian player 58th overall in the 2011 NHL Entry Draft.

After that, Kucherov played major junior hockey with the Rouyn-Noranda Huskies of the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League(QMJHL). Following the draft, on September 10, 2012, he signed a three-year entry-level contract with Lightning.

Likewise, on 25 November 2013, Nikita scored his first shot against Henrik Lundqvist of the New York Rangers.

The following year against Mike Smith of the Arizona Coyotes, Kucherov scored his first career hat trick. But the 2014-15 season was his breakout season where Nikit posted 29 goals, 36 assists, and 65 points.

On May 20, Kucherov joined Martin St. Louis as the only player in Tampa history to record two game-winning goals in overtime.

Then on October 11, 2016, Tampa Bay re-signed the young athlete to a three-year contract worth $4.766 million per season. He scored and made many career-high goals throughout the season while also skating for the 17 Stanley Cup playoff.
