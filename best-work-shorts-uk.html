10 best work shorts UK 2023 including Carhartt and Dickies

 Posted on July 18, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1193 words  |  Aldo Pusey

WHETHER you’re after some shorts for your actual job, or you just want a utility-inspired pair for your weekend wardrobe, adding a pair of work shorts to your rotation is a smart move.

Usually made from hard-wearing cotton or lightweight, waterproof technical fabrics, work shorts are comfortable, practical and surprisingly versatile. Coming with a pocket or two on the thigh, and with flattering relaxed fits, they will keep you cool on-site and will add a rugged edge to your summer casualwear. From where to find the right pair to how to style them and what to look out for, here’s everything you need to know about this underrated wardrobe essential. 

Things to consider 

What are work shorts? 

Work shorts are very similar to cargo shorts. They typically come with side pockets, either in the form of expandable bellows pockets, flap pockets or patch pockets. Typically these are designed for holding tools, documents or other daily essentials since work shorts are primarily intended to be worn for manual, outdoor work. And that’s the difference with cargos. Where cargo shorts do have pockets, they are typically worn for fashion, whereas work shorts are a bit more hardcore. 

Work shorts will also usually be made from a super durable fabric; usually cotton blended with a polyester or nylon to give it added strength. They might also be treated with a water-repellent coating, and will nearly always be abrasion or tear resistant. In short, if you need a pair of shorts that will have your back on a building site or carpenters workshop, or if you want to add a technical, seriously utilitarian feel to your look, this is the only way to go. 

How to style them 

You can wear work shorts in much the same way as you would a pair of cargos. The two styles look very similar and therefore wear in very similar ways. They’re an inherently casual piece and will make a great addition to your weekend attire. Go for a relaxed-fitting pair and wear them with boxy T-shirts or polo shirts, and finish with a pair of canvas trainers or retro running shoes. Alternatively, you could dress them up slightly by wearing them with a Cuban collar short-sleeve shirt, and complete the look with chunky boat shoes.
