More on Donnie Baker

Ron Sexton was known for playing Donnie Baker on The Bob & Tom Show as well as taking the character on the road.

Baker was a spoof character and as described on his website: "Straight from parts unknown... harvested from soggy bottom bayou where a free range wolf just laid it's eggs..."

Baker's catch phrase was: "Beating rabies & making babies."

Loyal fans honor Ron Sexton

Many loyal Bob & Tom fans have flocked to the show's Facebook page to share their love for Ron Sexton.

One comment read: "Im now 32. I’ve been listening since the 4th grade. The years I’ve spent with you guys is so special to me and I’m proud to say Ron was one of the biggest reasons I’ve stayed a fan so long! He will be missed but he will never be forgotten!"

Another wrote: "Rest in Peace Ron. You made one of a kind characters who will be remembered forever."

"Thank you so much for showing us the man behind the bit. It's so wonderful to have these things to look back on and laugh.

I'm sure Mr. Sexton is listening to our laughter from heaven knowing he left a shinning light in many lives and will for years to come through his recordings. My heart goes out to all of you, from his work family to his regular family, to his fan family," a third added.

The Bob & Tom Show honors Ron Sexton, part two

The Bob & Tom Show compiled video footage of Sexton on the show for a tribute to the late comedian.

The caption read: "Ron Sexton is best known for Donnie Baker but voiced many other characters for our show.

"If you look through the Donnie Baker playlist on YouTube, you'll see dozens of videos on-air and behind the scenes at the BOB & TOM Show.

"Whether on stage, on the air, in a promo for a local radio station, in front of a band, or just in conversation, much of what Ron said was improvised.

"We'd prompt him, and he would come up with something fantastic, usually in one take. We wanted to give you a glimpse of the immense talent we have been privileged to watch for over 20 years, and one we will greatly miss as his friends and coworkers."

The Bob & Tom show honors Ron Sexton

The Bob & Tom Show posted a video montage of Ron Sexton on their Facebook page.

The video included multiple clips of Sexton filming impressions from the show, including one of Tony Soprano.

His personality shined during the behind-the-scenes footage where he can be seen laughing and talking with the crew.

Ron Sexton died at age 52

Comedian Ron Sexton has died at the age of 52.

His cause of death has not yet been revealed.

Ron’s best friend pays heartbreaking tribute, continued

The tribute continues: “It was a sight to behold when Ron stepped on stage, transforming into Donnie Baker, the lovable character that captured the hearts of audiences everywhere.

“But it was behind the scenes where Ron’s true nature shone brightly. He would spend endless hours after each show, making sure he met every fan who wanted to connect with him.

“It didn’t matter if it took all night; Ron was determined to show his gratitude and reciprocate the love he received.”
