Short Bio On Jason Sehorn

Jason Sehorn, originally Jason Heath Sehorn, is a former American football cornerback who played professionally in the National Football League.

He played for the New York Giants for nine seasons from 1994 to 2002 and St. Louis Rams in 2003.

Born in Sacramento, California, Sehorn attended Mount Shasta High School in Mount Shasta, California.

After playing one year of high school football, Sehorn played his first two years of college football at Shasta College, a junior college in Redding, California.

He then played at the University of Southern California for two years.

After completing college, the New York Giants of the NFL drafted Sehorn in the second round of the 1994 NFL Draft. Jason played cornerback for the Giants for most of his time on the team.

He represented the Giants in Super Bowl XXXV, which they lost to the Baltimore Ravens.

On March 7, 2003, Giants released Sehorn, and in May that same year, St. Louis Rams signed him as a safety.

However, because of a broken foot, he missed the first six games of the season.

The following year his deal with the Rams concluded, and since he failed a physical examination before the start of the 2004 season, he was terminated by the team.
