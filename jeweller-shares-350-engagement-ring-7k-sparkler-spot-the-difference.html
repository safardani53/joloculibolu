IF old wedding traditions are anything to go by, your perfect engagement ring was always supposed to cost at least three months' salary.

But whether you're dreaming of a ring fit for a princess or fancy a more understated sparkler, a professional jeweller has now revealed exactly how you can tell a budget-friendly ring from a bank-breaking design.

Australian jeweller Daniel Anania of Anania Jewellers shared a photo of a cubic zirconia engagement ring worth £270 next to a £6,000 diamond design with the Daily Mail.

With a near identical platinum band and similar-shaped carat, it's almost impossible to spot the real diamond from the extremely convincing fake.

Highlighting the very subtle differences between the two, Daniel revealed that the fogginess of the stone usually indicates that it's a real diamond.

The jeweller said: "If the stone is too clean inside and is being sold too cheaply it is probably fake."

Meanwhile, Daniel pointed out that real diamond rings also come with a laser inscription on the band with their certificate number.

And if this wasn't enough, the weight of the band is usually a giveaway for whether it cost a small fortune or not.

The jeweller recommended: "Feel the weight. Silver is light, gold is heavy and platinum is even heavier."

That said, cubic zirconia is actually TWICE as heavy as a real diamond and scratches far more easily.

How can you tell cubic zirconia from diamonds?

  • Zirconia is TWICE as heavy as a diamond
  • Zirconia is a clearer stone while diamonds are often foggier
  • Zirconia scratches far easier
  • If you shine a light on zirconia, it will reflect a rainbow of different colours
