Supermodel Bella Hadid Endorsed Mica Arganarazs Contribution Resolution For Ukraine

 Posted on June 5, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  351 words  |  Martina Birk

Mica Arganaraz, an Argentine model, has revealed her intentions to donate to Ukraine. Bella Hadid, a supermodel, backed the model’s resolve. The model desires to assist her model colleagues, friends, and anybody else who is suffering during these difficult times. She said that this was something she could do from her end. On February 27, the 29-year-old model shared the following statement on Instagram:

“I have to admit, it’s strange walking fashion shows knowing there’s a war going on on the same continent.”

She went on, stating,

“I will donate a portion of my profits from this fashion week to Ukrainian organizations.”

Along with Bella Hadid, model Kaia Gerber supported her coworker Mica Arganaraz. Bella replied to the model, saying, “You said it, @micaarganaraz,” she said.

Everything about Mica Arganaraz

Mica Arganaraz is a social media personality in addition to being a model. Throughout her career, she has been the face of several major companies, like Calvin Klein, H&M, Mango, and Versace, to mention a few. Mica is also regarded as an industry icon. On May 16, 1992, the model was born in Argentina’s capital, Buenos Aires. Despite having studied cinematography, Mica entered the modeling profession after getting an offer from a well-known modeling agency in Argentina.

Arganaraz began her career as a model for Christopher Kane’s winter and fall collections in 2013. By 2015, she has started to work with world-renowned designers like Tom Ford, Michael Kors, Lacoste, and Alexander Wang. Furthermore, the model has collaborated with some high-end fashion houses, including Louis Vuitton, Dior, Givenchy, and Chanel. Mica Arganaraz has also been on the covers of Vogue, Elle, Glamor, People, W, GQ, and Harper’s Bazaar, among others.

Bella Hadid’s reaction to the situation

In reaction to Arganaraz’s pledge, Bella described walking in fashion shows in these times as “strange and unusual.” Bella urged individuals in the fashion business to speak out and have open talks about the world’s continuing conflicts. She described fashion as a lonely industry where one feels like an outcast. Many other models, like Kiki Willems, Vittoria Ceretti, Francesca Summers, and Aylah Peterson, came out supporting Mica.
