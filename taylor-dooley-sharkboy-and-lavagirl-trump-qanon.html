Is Sharkboy and Lavagirl star Taylor Dooley a Trump supporter?

 Posted on February 3, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  566 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

ACTRESS Taylor Dooley, who played Lavagirl in 2005's The Adventures of Sharkboy and Lavagirl, is a Trump supporter, according to her social media.

Eagle-eyed fans noticed Dooley had liked several Trump and QAnon-supporting tweets in light of the announcement of the film's sequel We Can Be Heroes.

Who is Taylor Dooley?

Taylor Dooley is an actress and student.

She started acting at a young age and landed the role of Lava Girl alongside Taylor Lautner in Robert Rodriquez's film The Adventures of Sharkboy and Lavagirl.

After the film debuted in 2005, Dooley took a break from acting. Years later, she opted to go back to college and study psychology.

"To me, what I love about acting is understanding people and what makes them tick, which is what makes Psych so interesting," she wrote in her IMDB biography.

"Growing up a child actor is somewhat of a focused and sheltered life. I wanted to experience so much more. So, needless to say, these years away have been the best 'life road trip' I could have taken."

"It not only helped me develop and grow so much as a person, but my experiences helped me grow as an actress."

Why do fans think she supports Trump?

Screenshots of her Twitter show that the actress has appeared to have liked various tweets in support of US President Donald Trump.

One tweet read: "Will you please pray for President Trump? He needs us now more than ever."

Another said: "This is the man that is fighting for good and fighting for us. We need to continue to pray and fight! 88 day to go."

Dooley also liked pro-police tweets and a tweet that declared that Ivanka Trump and the President are "fully committed to ending human trafficking once and for all."

After fans called her out, she appeared to have unlike the tweets.

Her social media suggests she also believes in far-right QAnon conspiracies, which alleges that Democratic Satan-worshiping pedophiles are running a global sex-trafficking ring.

Dooley's conspiracist likes highlight that she thinks John F. Kennedy is alive and that he too is a Trump supporter who's hiding out in Pennsylvania.

What has she said about her social media?

Dooley has not addressed her Twitter likes publicly.

She has not openly spoken about her political affiliations.
