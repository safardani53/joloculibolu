Record-breaking horse trainer shocks racing by quitting and says 'I've got sheep to look after'

 Posted on February 4, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  683 words  |  Martina Birk

A RECORD-BREAKING horse trainer who's won millions has shocked racing by handing in his licence.

Keith Dalgleish, won also won at Royal Ascot as a jockey, delivered the bombshell and said: "I've got sheep that need looking after."

Dalgleish, 39, has won just shy of £3million over the past five seasons on the Flat alone, with another £800,000 coming over jumps.

He insisted the decision to stop training at his South Lanarkshire base was nothing to do with money - but that he just wanted to do something else.

Dalgleish, whose top horses include Aberama Gold and What's The Story, broke the record for most winners in a Flat season by a Scottish-based trainer five times.

And with 952 career wins over both codes, he was always a popular choice with owners.

But he insisted now was the time to pack up - for a while at least.

Dalgleish, who had a 100-1 winner at Newcastle just last month, told the Racing Post: "I'm going to stop training this year.
