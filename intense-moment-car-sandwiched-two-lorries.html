A VIDEO has revealed the moment a car is nearly sandwiched between two lorries - but viewers are divided on who's in the wrong.

The dashcam footage shows a blue Honda CR-V struggling to join the motorway from a slip road, appearing unsure of the speed of the traffic in the outer lanes.

At the same time, a lorry on the inside lane of the slip road continues on and blocks the Honda from the left.

People on social media were quick to blame the car driver, with most saying he should have adjusted his approach speed much better as he joined the motorway.

The Highway Code appears to agree, saying: "When you join the motorway, you will normally approach it from a road on the left (a slip road) or from an adjoining motorway.

"You should give priority to traffic already on the motorway, check the traffic on the motorway and match your speed to fit safely into the traffic flow in the left-hand lane.

"Do not cross solid white lines that separate lanes or use the hard shoulder.

"Stay on the slip road if it continues as an extra lane on the motorway.

"And also remain in the left-hand lane long enough to adjust to the speed of traffic before considering overtaking."
