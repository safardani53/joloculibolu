Coronavirus outbreak at French nudist resort sees 240 test positive &amp; 800 quarantined after raunchy

 Posted on September 8, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  664 words  |  Martina Birk

A STARTLING 240 out of 800 naturists at a secluded French nudist village with swinger clubs are infected with coronavirus, says a report.

The huge spike follows a raunchy roof-top party at a plush Cap d’Agde hotel, where no social distancing measures took place, its boss admits.

Health bosses set up mobile testing outside the naked village - a few miles from Montpellier - and found that 30 per cent of 800 nudists checked tested positive for Covid-19, says the BBC.

This shocking figure is four times higher than in neighbouring areas in the south coast of France on the Mediterranean Sea.

The enclosed, self-contained "village naturiste" offers visitors about a dozen swinger clubs and saunas, plus erotic nightclubs, the broadcaster adds.

Cap d’Agde boasts that it’s “one of the best resorts of its type in the world for sunbathing in the buff” and the population can swell to “50,000 at any one time”.

One unnamed swinger couple told the BBC: "We all know why we are here.

"There are plenty of other more traditional, family-based naturist camps elsewhere along the coast without the sex clubs".

The recent spike in coronavirus cases started in late August, and it's been tracked back to two employees at the luxury Hotel Oz'Inn.

Its owner said that an X-rated party was hosted on the hotel's roof terrace - without social distancing measures.

One naked couple, in their sixties, told the BBC that because of their age they were "more cautious" about "how many people we are in close contact with".

But, they claim, the outbreak is attributed to the "younger crowd who have taken more risks".

David Masella, who manages the village, said Cap d’Agde had been "hit doubly hard".

He added: "Forty per cent of our visitors are foreign; most come from the Netherlands and Germany, followed by Italians and the British".

Masella pointed out that the village's population density is "seven times greater than nearby Montpellier" as it has 10,000 campsite pitches, and accommodation within Cap d'Agde offers 15,000 beds.

A spokesman for the Hérault Prefecture said: "We are in an alarming situation.

"We also ask all the people who wish to come to this naturist village to postpone their arrival."

On its Facebook, Cap d'Agde - now in quarantine - announced that 17 establishments have closed in the naturist village to stop the Covid-19 from rampaging further.

The Regional Health Agency warned of the "very worrying" circulation of the coronavirus after registering nearly 30 per cent positive cases on just a single day last week, the village added.

Bars, restaurants, and night clubs are among the businesses temporarily shuttered as a result.

Regional health officials have urged the village to undertake "strict compliance with preventive measures and barriers".

President of the Cap-d'Agde hotel club, Frédéric Puech, said: "It would have been good if more stringent sanitary measures were put in place in the naturist village from the start of the summer, given the large number of holidaymakers who were going to stay there.

"Some are responsible for this crisis, but many are not, unfortunately, and the result is there."

He suggested that flowers should be sent to the owner of Hotel Oz'Inn, adding "Thanks to him the season will be a disaster for the whole of Cape Town."

At the naked village, people visit "the bank, post office, restaurants, launderette or for a walk along the mile-long beach, without a shred of clothes", according to its website.

"In areas such as the camping area, many people stay naked 24 hours a day," it adds.
