Ben Askren: Early Life, Family, and Education

Ben was born in Cedar Rapids, the second-largest city in Iowa, United States, on July 18, 1984. He was born to Chuck Askren and Michele Askren. Furthermore, he spent his childhood with his brother Max Askren.

Like him, Max is also a wrestler as a professional who won a national championship in 2010. By the way, Ben was born and raised Christian.

Next, talking about his education, Ben went to Arrowhead High School, where he completed his high school education.

Notably, he won the state championship in wrestling two times at the school. Then, he enrolled at the University of Missouri.

At the same time, he worked as an assistant wrestling coach at Arizona State University. In his college days, he collected many honorable awards in wrestling, which served as a boost in his professional career.

Ben Askren: Wrestling Career

He was in 174 lb. weight class during his collegiate wrestling career. Significantly, as an amateur wrestler in his university.

He won the National Collegiate Athletic Association Championship match in 2006 and 2007 after consecutive defeats in the same competition in 2004 and 2005. He still holds the record for a single-season NCAA pin.

Moreover, he won gold medals in both the 2006 and 2007 Big 12 Championships. In the 2006-2007 season, he went undefeated with a record of 42-0.

Also, he won the Big 12 Conference Championship in 2004, 2006, and 2007. He was famous for his unorthodox ‘funky’ style of wrestling and his pinning skills during those times.

He finished his wrestling career at the college with an impressive 87 match winning streak with a record of 153-8.

Explicitly, Oklahoma St.’s Chris Pendleton and Purdue’s Ryan Lange are the only two opponents who defeated Ben during his four years at Missouri.

Olympics 2008

Similarly, he qualified for the 2008 Summer Olympics by defeating national runner-up Tyrone Lewis at the US Olympic Trials. And, competing in the 74 kg weight class, he won his first match.

However, he had to lose out from the competition without any medal after his defeat in the round-of-eight match to Cuba’s Ivan Fundora and Fundora’s loss in the quarterfinal.  After the game, Ben commented,

“I just wasn’t good enough. I sucked.”

As a consequence, Ben halted his wrestling career. However, he took part in wrestling on some occasions.

After that, he won the fight against Cuba’s Fundora in the Cerro Pelado International semifinals in 2010 to requite his defeat in Olympics.

Furthermore, he took part in the Agon Wrestling Championships and the Flo Premier League.

Ben Askren: MMA career

After the Olympics, Ben started fighter training at the American Top Team’s main school in Florida. On February 7, 2009, he made his professional MMA debut in Columbia, where he defeated Josh Flowers.

After that, his second fight was at Patriot Act 2 in Columbia, where he defeated Mitchell Harris. Then, he took part in the 2009 ADCC Submission Wrestling World Championship in Barcelona.

However, Ben was knocked out of the competition by Pablo Popovitch in the second match after winning against Toni Linden. Later he won gold in the 84 kg division at the 2009 FILA 2o World Grappling Championship.

Bellator Fighting Championship

Then, Ben took part in Bellator Fighting Championships Season 2 Welterweight Tournament. At Bellator 14 and the rematch at Bellator 19, Ben defeated Ryan Thomas.

He was then crowned as the champion of the competition at Bellator 22 after defeating Dan Hornbuckle for all three rounds. He defended the title for four successive years at Bellator 40, 56, 64, and 86.

The dominant win against Andrey Koreshkov at Bellator 97 was the final fight of his contract before the contract terminated at the company in November of 2013.

ONE Championship

Following the release, he signed a 2-year contract with ONE Championship. Ben defeated Bakhtiyar Abbasov, then with a nine-fight win streak, in the ONE Fighting Championship: Honor and Glory event on May 30.

Later he became the new ONE Welterweight Champion on August 29, 2014, following the win against Nobutastsu Suzuki at ONE FC: Reign of Champions.

He won all the six fights under his contract with one fight ruled out as “no contest.”

UFC

Another key thing, Ben signed for the UFC on November 3, 2018, under the “trade UFC and ONE Championship agreement”.

Notably, on his UFC debut, he had a controversial win against the former UFC Welterweight Champion Robbie Lawler at UFC 235.

”When someone decides to come into the UFC, and they don’t have a combat sports background, you’re kind of thinking, ‘Does he understand what he’s getting into?”

Later, he faced two defeats: the fastest knockout in UFC history against Jorge Masvidal and ‘Fight of the Night’ award-winning loss against Demian Maia. At last, he retired from the MMA competition on November 18, 2019.

Ben Askren: Achievements & Awards

Askren pinned in 25 of 45 matches he won in his 2005-2006 collegiate season, which earned him the Schalles Award Top Collegiate Pinner of the Year in 2006 and 2007.

There he was National Collegiate Athletic Association Division I Champion in 2006 and 2007 and NCAA Division I All-American Finalist for four consecutive years since 2004.

He was awarded Dan Hodge Trophy for Collegiate Wrestler of the Year in 2006 and 2007. He was presented in the Intercollegiate Athletics Hall of Fame by the University of Missouri in 2012.

Similarly, Askren won gold medals at the 2005 Pan American Championships Senior Freestyle, 2007 and 2009 Hargobind International Tournament Senior Freestyle,

2007 NYAC Holiday International Open Senior Freestyle, 2010 Dave Schultz Memorial International Open Senior Freestyle, and most notably in FILA 2009 Grappling World Championships Senior No-Gi.

He won USA Senior Freestyle National Championship in 2008 and FILA World Team Trials Senior No-Gi in 2009.

Moreover, Ben won Bellator Season 2 Welterweight Championship. Also, he has held the Bellator record for defending the title for most successive years (four).

Moreover, he has also successfully defended the title of ONE Welterweight Champion three times after winning it in 2014.

Disc Golf

Apart from these, Ben is also a competitive player in the sport of disc golf. For instance, he secured second place in the 2011 United States Amateur Disc Golf Championship,

First place at 2009 Mighty MO Advanced, and third place in 2006 Alabama Amateur Open Intermediate and 2009 First Class Challenge.

Along with these, he came fourth in the 2009 PDGA Amateur World Doubles Championships Advanced.
