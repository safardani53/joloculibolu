Little Couple's Jen Arnold reveals heartbreaking family update &amp; says the 'world won't be the same'

 Posted on January 1, 0001  |  3 minutes  |  487 words  |  Aldo Pusey

LITTLE Couple star Dr. Jen Arnold has revealed a heartbreaking family update on social media.

The TLC star took to Instagram to inform her followers that the family's dog, Rocky, has passed away.

Jen shared sweet photos of their 14-year-old Chihuahua resting surrounded by pillows and blankets.

In one, the reality star cradles the pup in her arms as he looks to be half asleep.

In her lengthy post, Jen wrote: "Our hearts are broken & the world will never be the same.

"Our dearest #Rocky has left us."

Jen explained that Rocky inspired her and her husband Bill Klein to start their own pet boutique, Rocky and Maggies, in Houston, Texas.

"His legacy will live on forever just as he will live on forever in our hearts," she explained.

Thousands of fans expressed their condolences on Instagram.

One wrote: "So very sorry for your loss... Praying for you children and y'all."

The tragic announcement comes after Jen took to the platform the share the upsetting news that Rocky was in the hospital.

Along with a photo of her daughter cuddling the adult dog, she wrote: “Please pray for the best Chihuahua and friend anyone could ever have,” she began.

“Rocky is in the ICU and we are praying that he spends a little bit more time with us. 

“We know the world loves Rocky as much as we do.”

Jen's devastated fans shared their prayers in the comments.

“Oh, praying with you, my friend,” wrote one.

Fans are currently waiting to find out whether the couple will ever return to TLC as their show The Little Couple has been on hiatus since 2019.

Although many fans are hoping that the reality series will make a comeback, the clues hint that it’s not likely to happen anytime soon.

However, Jen and Bill will appear on an upcoming episode of HGTV's Farmhouse Fixer after they moved into a massive $2million Boston mansion earlier this year.

The reality star used the clip to poll fans on whether or not her son is ready for his own social media accounts.

Music played over the TikTok clip, which showed Will, 12, nodding his head affirmatively while Jen wagged her finger, gesturing "No."

She shared the TikTok video on Instagram, adding a question box that read: "Is Will ready for TikTok?"

It's unclear, at least at the moment, how fans responded.

As of late, fans have been commenting on how grown-up the kids look.

Will is on the cusp of his teenage years, with sister Zoey not far behind.
