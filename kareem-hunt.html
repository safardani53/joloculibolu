Kareem Hunt | Early Career

Kareem played for the Rebels football team while at Willoughby South High School. He rushed for 2,519 yards and made 39 touchdowns as a junior.

Further, it increased to 2,685 yards and 44 touchdowns in his senior year. Hunt was rated as a three-star recruit by Rivals.com. 

In 2013, Hunt played in 12 games. These games had three starts and rushed for 866 yards on 137 carries with six touchdowns. Moreover, he also became the MVP of the 2015 GoDaddy Bowl.

However, in 2015, College Sports Department suspended Hunt for the first two games due to a violation of team rules.

Later, he returned for the third game, but the injury caused him to lose the other three games instead.

Overall, in 2016 he showed a great performance. He also holds career school records for yards per rush at 6.3, with a minimum of 100 jets.

Kareem Hunt | Professional Career

Kansas City Chiefs

On September 7, 2017, Hunt made his NFL debut. Hunt’s position for the team was running back. For his outstanding performance, the organizers have given him many awards.

Likewise, Hunt remained the Chiefs’ primary running back going into the 2018 season. However, Hunt was fined $26,739 for a hit where he intentionally lowered his helmet.

Hunter continued his great momentum in every week’s game. During Week 11, Hunt rushed for 28 yards and caught six passes for 41 yards and a touchdown.

Further, Hunt was released by the Chiefs on November 30, 2018, after a videotape of him physically assaulting a woman surfaced on the internet.

Cleaveland Browns

On February 11, 2019, the Cleveland Browns signed Hunt. At that time, his assault case investigation was pending. However, on March 15, 2019, NFL suspended Hunt from the first eight games 2019.

Later he returned and started practicing with the team again on October 21. Finally, on November 4, 2019, he made his first appearance with the Browns in Week 10.

On week 12, he scored his first touchdown of the season, along with a 6-yard rush. Hunt finished the 2019 season with 179 rushing yards, two rushing touchdowns, 285 receiving yards, and a receiving touchdown.

Further, the Cleaveland Browns offered a free-agent tender on Hunt on March 16, 2020. Later in April, he signed the offer.

Again, on September 8, 2020, the Browns team signed Kareem to a two-year contract, including a $13.25 million contract extension.

For the 2020 season Week 2, Hunt rushed 10 times for 86 yards, his first rushing touchdown and first receiving the season’s touchdown.
