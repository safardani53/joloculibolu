Clara Amfo Age & Early Life

Clara Amfo was born on May 28, 1984, in Kington in Thames, England. She is of All-American descent with Ghanaian ancestors. Emmanuel, her father, was a scientist and pathologist by trade. He is originally from Ghana. In 2015, he died of a heart attack. Grace Amfo is the name of her mother. Andy is her brother and sister.

Moving on to studies, she finished high school at Holy Cross School. She previously attended Burntwood School in Wandsworth, South London. She later earned an Honors in Arts degree from St Mary’s University College and focused on professional and creative writing.

Career

Clara Amfo began her work at KISS FM as a marketing intern. She used to host the evening program at the time. Following the complexion, she joined the Hollywood world as a Nickelodeon presenter. She got a Sony Radio Award in the emerging star category for her efforts. She had already entered the BBC Talent competition at that point. As a consequence, she joined BBC Radio 1Xtra as a weekend morning show presenter in September 2013.

The following year, she became the presenter of MTV’s weekly official UK Top 40. In addition, she presented The Official Chart Update. She then featured on the BBC’s Innuendo Bingo radio program. She presented The Offical Chart on BBC Radio 1 in 2015. The same year, she took over Fearne Cotton’s BBC Radio 1 mid-morning programme when Fearne had to leave due to her pregnancy. She used to host the show’s Live Lounge portions.

She narrated the British reality game show Coach Trip for the channel E4 in July 2016. Following that, she began hosting award shows such as the BRIT Awards, The Year in Music 2017, Top of the Pops, and others. She also presented the Hammersmith Apollo premieres of films such as Red Riding Hood, Baby Driver, Tomb Raider, and Black Panther. On her mid-morning broadcast in June 2020, she delivered an impassioned monologue about George Floyd’s death and prejudice. She received a lot of acclaim for it.

It was announced on September 2, 2020, that she would compete in the dance reality program Strictly Come Dance. The program will premiere on October 17, 2020. She has hosted TV series such as The One Show, Top of the Pops, and Love Island: Aftersun in addition to radio broadcasts. Amfo won the challenge on episode 1 of the relaunched series The Weakest Link in December 2021. She also appeared as a guest judge on the Snatch Game episode of Rupaul’s Drag Race: UK vs. the World on February 22, 2022.
