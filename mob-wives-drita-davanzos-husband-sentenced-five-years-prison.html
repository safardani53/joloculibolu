Mob Wives star Drita DAvanzos husband Lee sentenced to five years in prison on gun charges

 Posted on January 30, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  369 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

THE Husband of Mob Wives star Drita D’Avanzo as been sentenced to five years in prison on gun charges.

Lee D’Avanzo, a twice-convicted bank burglar, was handed 64 months - nearly 20 more than prosecutors had asked for - by federal Judge Rachel Kovner for being a felon in possession of a gun.

His counsel asked for 37 months.

"The defendant had two guns in the house with his kids, and they were loaded with hollow-point ammunition," the judge said.

"At the time, there was other contraband in the house."

It was also reported that she "didn't have confidence Lee would learn his lesson if she gave him a lighter sentence."

The judge also referenced his six prior convictions and previous violations for supervised release.

Drita, 44, wrote a letter to the judge in which she praised the 51-year-old as an "amazing father" and that Gizelle and Aleeya were "hoping he comes home soon."

The reality star and their two daughters were all in court.

Investigators searched their Staten Island home in late 2019 and found two Smith & Wesson firearms loaded with hollow-point bullets, one in the cabinet above the fridge and another their mattress.

120 pills of hydrocodone/acetaminophen, 22 Xanax tablet, scales with residue and Ziplock bags were found in the home, along with 16oz of marijuana were also found in the kitchen.

Both Lee and Drita were charged with criminal possession of a controlled substance, criminal possession of a weapon and acting in a manner injurious to a child.

Staten Island prosecutors later dropped 17 felony and misdemeanor counts.

Drita previously appeared on VH1 show Mob Wives, on which she alleged that police believe her husband to be "the leader of a Bonanno and Colombo crime family farm team."
