CELEBRITIES who shared intimate snaps with their loved ones have been targeted in recent years by hackers who leaked the photos online.

A number of the horrified stars have lashed out, with some even slamming the snaps shared online, as fake. Read on to find out who has been victimised...

What is an iCloud hack and who are the biggest victims?

iCloud celeb hacks first gained notoriety in the US on August 31 2014, when a tranche of personal photos belonging to female Hollywood celebs were posted online.

In what became known as "the Fappening", the most notable victims included actors Jennifer Lawrence, Kate Upton, Kaley Cuoco and Kirsten Dunst.

The snaps were stolen by web thieves who accessed Apple's iCloud — a storage platform which automatically backs up data on users' devices.

Investigations found that devious scams to get celebs to hand over passwords may have been used, as well as other targeted password attacks.

Hunger Games star Jen described the hack as a "sex crime", telling Vanity Fair: "Anybody who looked at those pictures, you're perpetuating a sexual offence and you should cower with shame".

In November 2017, the Oscar winner described the incident as "like being gang-banged by the f***ing planet".

Lawrence said the privacy invasion left her confidence shattered and in tears when people described her as a good role model.

She told the Hollywood Reporter: "When the hacking thing happened, it was so unbelievably violating that you can't even put it into words.

"I feel like I got gang-banged by the f***ing planet. There was not one person in the world that is not capable of seeing these intimate photos of me.

But fellow victim, model Emily Ratajkowski, said: "I'm not sure that anyone who Googles it is necessarily a criminal. I think the people who stole the photos are".

How have the celebrities responded?

In 2017, several stars were planning to take legal action against the hackers after successfully getting snaps removed from one site.

After a fake nude picture of X Factor judge Nicole Scherzinger appeared on a sleazy website, she asked lawyers to get them removed.

Olympic swimmer Lindsey Vonn, whom Tiger Woods dated for two years, had photos leaked online, along with a snap of the golf ace.

The pair’s lawyers successfully removed the pictures but they vowed to find the culprits.

Almost a year after her photos were leaked, WWE star Maria Kannelis took to Facebook to launch a scathing attack on the hackers.

Within a lengthy statement, she said: "I’m too sexy for small mindedness. I can and I will never be afraid of it.

"Being sexy doesn’t end for pregnant people, married people, curvy people, skinny people, any people… Sex is beautiful."

Apple responded by pledging to bolster iCloud security and an FBI investigation resulted in the March 2016 prosecution of Ryan Collins.

The 36-year-old from Lancaster, Pennsylvania pleaded guilty to a hacking charge and in October was sentenced to 12-months in prison.

In January 2016, Edward Majerczyk, 28, of Chicago, Illinois, was sentenced to nine months in prison for hacking an unnamed celeb.
